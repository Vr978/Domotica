Entrance Automation


Entrance Automation is a system that controls access to a building, room, or area by using technology. It provides security and convenience by allowing authorized individuals to enter while denying access to unauthorized persons. Here are some key points and examples of entrance automation:

► Types of systems: There are various types of entrance automation systems, including card readers, biometric scanners, keypads, security gates, turnstiles, and door access control systems.

► Security: Entrance automation systems increase security by requiring individuals to present identification, such as a card or biometric data, before being granted access

► Convenience: With entrance automation, authorized individuals can enter a building or area with ease and without the need for a physical key or manual check-in process.

► Integration: Some entrance automation systems can be integrated with other security systems, such as cameras or alarms, for additional security.

► Examples: Card readers: These systems require individuals to present a card, such as an ID card or a proximity card, to gain access.

► Biometric scanners: These systems use biometric data, such as fingerprints or iris scans, to identify individuals and grant access.

► Security gates: These systems physically block access to a building or area until authorized individuals present their identification.
