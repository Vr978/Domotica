Ventilation systems are systems designed to regulate the flow of air in and out of a building, maintaining indoor air quality and controlling the temperature and humidity of the space. Some common types of ventilation systems include:

► Natural ventilation: the use of open windows, doors, and vents to provide fresh air and improve indoor air quality.

► Mechanical ventilation: the use of fans, ducts, and other equipment to regulate the flow of air into and out of a building.

► HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) systems: a combination of heating, cooling, and ventilation systems used to regulate indoor air temperature, humidity, and air quality.

► Ventilation systems are important for maintaining indoor air quality and reducing the risk of airborne infections and other health hazards. They are also essential for improving comfort levels and energy efficiency in buildings.

► Ventilation systems are used in a wide range of buildings, including homes, offices, schools, hospitals, and factories. They are also used in vehicles, such as cars and trains, and in other enclosed spaces, such as tents and greenhouses.
